A Strategic Blueprint for National Transformation

This interactive report visualizes a sequential strategy for India's development. It argues that top-level economic growth is fundamentally dependent on strengthening the nation's foundational pillars: its people and its institutions. Explore the interconnected roadmap below.

Part I: Strengthening the Foundational Pillars

The journey begins by addressing the root causes: investing in the capabilities of citizens and the integrity of the state. These are the non-negotiable first steps for sustainable progress.

Human Capital: The Prerequisite for Progress

A skilled and healthy population is the primary input for a productive economy. This section explores the challenges and reforms in education, healthcare, and basic amenities.

State Capacity: The Bedrock of Trust

An efficient, transparent state that upholds the rule of law is the essential operating system for a modern economy. This requires tackling corruption and reforming administration and justice.

Part II: Powering the Economic Engine

With strong foundations, India can unleash its productive capacities. This requires modern infrastructure and revitalized core sectors to create jobs and drive growth.

Manufacturing's Share of GDP: Target vs. Reality

The 'Make in India' initiative aimed to increase manufacturing's contribution to GDP to 25%. However, it has remained stagnant. Success in this area is heavily dependent on foundational reforms.

Part III: Securing a Sustainable & Cohesive Future

Long-term prosperity depends on environmentally sustainable and socially inclusive growth. This requires balancing development with stewardship and addressing deep-seated inequalities.

The Inequality Paradox

While consumption data suggests equality, wealth is extremely concentrated. The top 1% holds over 40% of national wealth, while the bottom 50% holds just 3%. This creates a profound inequality of opportunity.

Green Energy Transition

India is a global leader in renewable energy, having already surpassed its 2030 target for non-fossil fuel capacity. The new goal is 500 GW by 2030, moving towards Net Zero by 2070.
